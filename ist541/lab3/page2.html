Origins of the Terrarium

The Accidental Invention of Terrariums

The terrarium was an accidental discovery by Nathaniel Bagshaw Ward, in 1820's London.

"It was Ward’s eureka moment. Tightly-sealed glass cases could be used to control humidity (very good for ferns) and air quality (also very good for ferns). Ward’s 1842 book Of the Growth of Plants in Closely Glazed Cases and the display of his cases at the 1851 Great Exhibition of the Works of Industry of All Nations publicized his exciting discovery." By Matthew Wills | JSTOR Daily (2018)
